You are enhancing existing collection pages by adding curated combination sections that help users visualize how products work together for real life moments and aesthetics.

Goal
Increase conversion by reducing decision fatigue and helping users see complete looks without overwhelming the page or disrupting the existing grid.

Do not redesign the page. Add lightweight, repeatable sections that sit naturally within the current layout.

Section concept

Each curated combination section represents a moment or use case, not a product bundle.

Examples of moments:

Moving into a small apartment

Cozy dorm room refresh

Calm evening wind down corner

First apartment living room

Minimal eco bedroom setup

Guest ready home refresh

Section structure (required)

Each curated combination section must include:

A short section title
Format:
"[Moment or aesthetic], made simple"

Examples:

"Moving into a small apartment, made simple"

"A cozy dorm corner, made simple"

"A calm evening wind down, made simple"

One short helper sentence
This explains why these pieces work together.

Examples:

"These pieces work together to add warmth, texture, and calm without taking up space."

"A balanced mix of lighting, texture, and soft accents for small rooms."

A horizontal product row or compact grid

Show 3 to 4 products only

These products already exist in the catalog

Do not create new product types or bundles

Maintain existing product card style

Optional subtle connector text above the products
Examples:

"Designed to layer together"

"Chosen to work as a set"

"Balanced for small spaces"

No buttons like “Shop the look.” The products themselves remain clickable.

Product selection rules

When creating a curated combination:

Choose products that naturally complement each other

Mix categories when appropriate (example: lighting + textile + decor)

Avoid price stacking with only high priced items

Do not repeat the same product in multiple curated sections on the same page

Typical mix:

One anchor item (lamp, rug, statement decor)

One soft texture (pillow, throw, pouf)

One grounding or functional item (plant, basket, small table)

Placement rules

Insert curated combination sections between existing collection sections, not at the very top

Limit to 1 or 2 curated sections per collection page initially

Do not push primary product grids too far down

Maintain existing typography and spacing system

Copy tone and constraints

Calm, helpful, and guiding

No sales language

No urgency

No emojis

No badges

No discounts

No “best seller” labels

Write like a trusted friend helping someone set up their space.

Acceptance criteria

Each collection page includes at least one curated combination section

Each section references a real life moment or aesthetic

Products feel intentionally chosen together

Page still feels lightweight and scrollable on mobile
